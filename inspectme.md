   Each page must satisfy a specific search intent better than a generic service page.

   Each page must add unique useful information, not just swap keywords.

   Pages must use real company proof, real capabilities, real process details, real technologies, and real examples. Do not invent fake clients, fake offices, fake reviews, fake certifications, or fake metrics.

   Important landing pages must receive internal links from the homepage, service hubs, industry hubs, blogs, and footer only where appropriate.

   Each page must clearly define:

   * Who Wortholic is
   * What service is offered
   * Who the service is for
   * What problem it solves
   * What process is used
   * What outcomes are realistic
   * How to contact Wortholic

9. Technical quality
   Pages must be fast, mobile-friendly, accessible, schema-valid, canonicalized, and free from broken links.

    Pages must convert visitors into leads with clear CTAs and low-friction forms.

Do not build pages only because a keyword exists.

────────────────────────────────

Build a scoring model for every possible page.

Score every page idea from 0 to 100.

Formula:

Opportunity Score =
25% Business Relevance

* 20% Search Intent Strength
* 15% Conversion Potential
* 10% Content Uniqueness
* 10% Competition Opportunity
* 10% Internal Linking Fit
* 5% Proof Availability
* 5% Technical Safety

Risk Score =
30% Duplicate Content Risk

* 25% Doorway Page Risk
* 20% Keyword Cannibalization Risk
* 15% Thin Content Risk
* 10% Unsupported Claim Risk

Index decision:

* Index if Opportunity Score >= 75 and Risk Score <= 30
* Draft if Opportunity Score 60–74 and Risk Score <= 45
* Noindex if Opportunity Score >= 60 but Risk Score 46–65
* Do not create if Opportunity Score < 60 or Risk Score > 65

SECTION 25 — ADVANCED GOOGLE-LEVEL SEO & E-E-A-T TACTICS (CTO ADDITIONS)
────────────────────────────────

To guarantee market dominance, we will elevate this strategy beyond basic programmatic SEO by implementing Semantic SEO, Entity mapping, and strict E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).

1. Semantic Entity Optimization (NLP & LSI)
Google's Natural Language Processing algorithms look for related entities, not just exact keywords.
- Every page must contain contextually relevant entities. For example, an "AI CRM" page must include terms like "customer data platform", "sales pipeline velocity", "lead scoring models", "predictive analytics", and "webhook integration".
- Use Google's NLP API mindset: Group entities into clusters and ensure the content naturally covers the entire topic graph.

2. E-E-A-T Enhancements
- Author Profiles: Emphasize leadership and technical expertise. Ensure the site highlights the real technical experience behind the brand.
- Case Study Schema: Mark up successful projects using structured data.
- Trust Signals: Emphasize "Data Privacy (GDPR/CCPA)", "HIPAA compliance for Healthcare", and "Enterprise-grade Security" across the site to build enterprise trust.

3. Zero-Click Search & Featured Snippet Optimization
- Add a "TL;DR" or "Key Takeaways" bulleted list at the top of long-form pages.
- Format FAQs exactly how Google extracts Featured Snippets (Question as H3, precise 40-60 word Answer paragraph immediately following).

4. Programmatic Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)
Traffic means nothing without leads. 
- Dynamic CTAs: The CTA must match the intent. If the user is on a "Healthcare Software" page, the CTA must be "Discuss Your Healthcare Software Project" rather than a generic "Contact Us".
- High-Value Offers: Offer a "Free AI Automation Audit" or "Free Architecture Review" to dramatically increase the conversion rate of incoming SEO traffic.
